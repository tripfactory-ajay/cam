Recommendations for Streamlining

1 — Activate the Daily Tracker
The Daily Tracker sheet already exists in the workbook. It should be populated daily per team member to give real-time visibility of uploads, audits, and live counts. Set a weekly reporting cadence into management.
2 — Resolve and Close Aged Pending Contracts
Assign ownership to each of the ~30 pending contracts, particularly the 10+ items pending since September–October 2025. Establish a maximum pending period (e.g. 30 days) with escalation if Supplier ID or documentation is not received.
3 — Plan for Sandhya's QC Handover
With Sandhya departing, redistribute her 156 QC assignments. Vasumitha or Sheetal are natural candidates given their existing QC exposure.
4 — Tour Mapping Handover Sprint (Before End-April 2026)
The 7 contractors leave in under 3 weeks. Prioritise: (a) clearing the 163 pending errors, (b) ensuring all mapped tours are audited and the audit column is marked, (c) documenting all unresolved system errors for the tech team.
5 — Create a Tracker for Sales Support Team
The 11-person Bangalore sales support team has no visible productivity data. Introduce a daily log covering: quotes assisted, Expedia/Agoda content tasks completed, static content updated.
6 — Consolidate Spreadsheet Tabs
41 tabs in CAM_INTERNAL.xlsx with multiple blank/orphaned sheets creates confusion. Recommend archiving 12+ empty tabs and standardising the structure around: (1) Upload Report, (2) QC, (3) Stop Sale, (4) Pending Queue, (5) Destination Reports.
7 — Verify Reported Headcount
Ajay was informed of 40–60 staff in contract loading. The sheets identify ~22. This gap should be clarified — either the additional staff are not tracked in these documents, or the number is inaccurate.
